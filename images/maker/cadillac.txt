Cadillac is a division of the American automobile manufacturer General Motors (GM) that designs and builds luxury vehicles. Its major markets are the United States, Canada, and China. Cadillac vehicles are distributed in 34 additional markets worldwide. Cadillac automobiles are at the top of the luxury field within the United States.[2] In 2017, Cadillac's U.S. sales were 156,440 vehicles and its global sales were 356,467 vehicles. It is among the primogenital automobiles company in the world, and second in the USA only to the companion GM marque Buick. The firm was discovered in 1902 from the remnants of the Henry Ford Company.
